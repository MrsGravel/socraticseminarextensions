Socratic Seminar Extension Menu

Complete your activities and submit online. Follow the instructions for each prompt carefully.

Student Name:

1. The Unheard Voices Analysis

Choose a character besides Melody. Analyze how they amplify or silence Melody’s voice. Provide a real-world example where someone’s voice was ignored or elevated. End with a thought-provoking question for discussion.

2. The Power of Words Micro-Essay

Explore how communication gives or takes power in the novel. Include at least 2 examples from the text and 1 real-world connection. Write a short essay (4-6 sentences) explaining your ideas.

3. The Hidden Curriculum Investigation

Examine the rules, expectations, or systems in Melody’s school. Make a short chart or list: who benefits, who is harmed. Write a reflection (3-5 sentences) explaining what Sharon Draper wants readers to understand about fairness and belonging.

4. Rewritten Moment Challenge

Choose a scene and rewrite it from a different character’s point of view or change a key decision. Explain in 3-5 sentences how this change alters the theme, message, or understanding of empathy.

5. Beyond Melody Synthesis Project

Pick a theme from the novel (e.g., communication, inclusion, courage). Connect it to a current event, TED Talk, or social movement. Explain your connection in 3-5 sentences and write a question to discuss in the seminar.
